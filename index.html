We Are Bangalore Chess Club

The Bangalore Chess Club, often referred to as the "Checkmate Capital of South India," is a vibrant and passionate community of chess enthusiasts in the heart of Bangalore, Karnataka. Established with the goal of promoting and nurturing the ancient and intellectually stimulating game of chess, this club has grown to become a hub for players of all ages and skill levels. With a rich history of hosting tournaments, organizing training sessions, and fostering a deep love for the game, the Bangalore Chess Club is where strategy meets camaraderie, and where knights, rooks, and queens come to life on the 64 squares. Whether you're a novice looking to learn the game or a seasoned grandmaster honing your skills, the Bangalore Chess Club welcomes all to be a part of its enduring legacy in the world of chess.

Rook and Roll

Bangalore chess club organized a Simul event featuring IM Rakesh Kulkarni a day before the FIDE World Championship 2023. Out of 20 players, Gurudeep Mathad and Nazeef Khan secured victories against the IM, while Vrajesh, Samaksh Ashok, and Rahil Shah managed draws, with the IM winning the Simul by 16.5-3.5.

World Chess Day

World Chess Day, established by UNESCO in 1966 and celebrated on July 20, promotes global chess growth. In Bengaluru, increased participation during the pandemic showcases rising interest, especially among children, with preparations for the Chess Olympiad in focus..
